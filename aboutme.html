Hello World!


My name is Milena and I am a fourth-year PhD student in the Department of Astronomy at the University of Maryland.

Prior to joining the Astronomy Department, I spent four years in Vermont, at Middlebury College, where I studied physics and mathematics. My research interests during the undergraduate years were two-fold: I studied physics on very small scales, specifically the attraction forces between two plates due to quantum fluctuations (Casimir effect, a.k.a. magic); and physics on large scales, by studying the co-evolution of galaxies and supermassive black holes at their centers. Coming to graduate school, I decided to further explore these two interests and combine them through my current research in dark matter.

I am most likely to be found in my office in PSC 1248, but ever so often I tunnel out to enjoy a good play, concert, crème brûlée, or a volleyball game (I play on the Astronomy Department IM team Dirty Snowballs!) I grew up on the Adriatic coast (Herceg-Novi, Montenegro), which probably explains my love for water sports, swimming in particular. Other interests include reading, creative writing, spoken word, (programming and human) languages and linguistics, math puzzles, NYT crossword puzzles, and drawing.

My homes are scattered across three continents and I am always planning a flight home.
