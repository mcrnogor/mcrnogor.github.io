The Delta Resonance in the Neutrino Sky
A. Khatee Zathul, K. Fang, F. Halzen, D. Hooper
arXiv:2606.00249  ·  astro-ph.HE
Why: Proposes that the ~30 TeV break in IceCube's diffuse neutrino spectrum arises from the Δ-resonance in p–γ interactions, which would also lower the neutrino-source contribution to the isotropic gamma-ray background.
Possible High-Energy Neutrino Emission from Dark Matter Annihilation in the Disrupting Dwarf Galaxy Boötes III
S. Ji, Z. Wang
arXiv:2606.01853  ·  astro-ph.HE
Why: Candidate high-energy neutrino signal from dark matter annihilation in the disrupting dwarf Boötes III, with a post-trial significance of 3.1σ pointing to a ~26.5 TeV dark matter mass.

25 MayA Precise Measurement of the Fermi-LAT Galactic Center Excess Morphology and Spectrum
M. Di Mauro (Fermi-LAT Collaboration)
arXiv:2605.22913  ·  astro-ph.HE
Why: An upgraded LAT analysis of the GCE across 9 IEMs that pushes residuals to ≲10% and finds a spherical gNFW (γ≈1.15) preferred over NB+BB bulge templates. At face value, interesting result. I am curious about the iterative "find_sources" step, that (I think) is run with the NFW in place with extensions allowed up to 2 deg---these are comparable to the angular scales of the boxy bulge wings for example.
22 MayA Three-Dimensional Tomographic Reconstruction of the Galactic Cosmic-Ray Proton Density
H. Zandinejad et al.
arXiv:2605.22739  ·  astro-ph.HE
Why: A nice step toward 3D Galactic CR tomography beyond traditional ring analyses, though the physical interpretation of the 4–6 kpc enhancement remains assumption-dependent.
