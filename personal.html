Before the age of sixteen, I changed three home countries without changing my residential address. Then I moved to another country. At eighteen years old, I moved again. At twenty-two, again.

I was born and raised in a small town on the coast of Federal Republic of Yugoslavia (1994–2002), or Serbia and Montenegro (2002–2006), or Montenegro (2006–2011). In 2011, I hopped on a plane by myself and left my home country to attend Li Po Chun United World College in Hong Kong, a high school with two hundred students from more than ninety countries, on a full merit-based scholarship. In September of 2013, I moved to Vermont, USA, as a Davis UWC Scholar to study at Middlebury College where I obtained my undergraduate degree. Finally, in 2017, I moved to Maryland, where I have lived ever since. Being a member of different communities constantly reshaped my personality by putting various parts of my identity forward and, in turn, strongly influencing my development as a scientist.

Nowadays, I am most likely to be found in my office in PSC 1248, but ever so often I tunnel out to enjoy a good play, concert, crème brûlée, or a volleyball game (I play on the Astronomy Department IM team Dirty Snowballs!) I grew up on the Adriatic coast (Herceg-Novi, Montenegro), which probably explains my love for water sports: I am an avid swimmer and love everything sea-related. Early in the morning ~four times a week, you'll find me at the pool, swimming with the Terrapin Masters Team (our relay made the 2nd best time in the US for our age group in 2019!) Other interests include reading, creative writing, spoken word, (programming and human) languages and linguistics, math puzzles, NYT crossword puzzles, and drawing.
